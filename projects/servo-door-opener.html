📖 Introduction

Servo motors are the entry point to robotics and automation. Unlike continuous DC motors, a hobby servo moves to a precise angle (0°–180°) and holds it — making it perfect for doors, gates, and steering mechanisms. The SG90 micro servo is the go-to component for beginners: cheap, 5 V tolerant, and strong enough for cardboard/foam structures.

In this project you'll trigger a door to open on a button press and automatically close after a delay. You can then upgrade the trigger to a PIR sensor, RFID reader, or ultrasonic proximity check — turning it into a real automatic door or pet flap.

⚙️ How It Works

  1. 1
    Wire the servo — Connect servo signal wire to pin 9 (PWM). Include Servo.h in your sketch, declare Servo myServo;, call myServo.attach(9); in setup(), then myServo.write(0); to initialise the closed position.
  2. 2
    Read the button — Wire a push-button to pin 2 with a 10 kΩ pull-down resistor. Use digitalRead(2) and debounce with a 50 ms delay or millis() logic to avoid false triggers.
  3. 3
    Sweep open and hold — On button press, call myServo.write(90); (or your chosen open angle) and record openTime = millis(). The door stays open while a boolean isOpen is true.
  4. 4
    Auto-close — Check millis() - openTime > HOLD_MS (e.g. 5000). When true, sweep back: myServo.write(0); and reset isOpen = false. Adjust angles and link arm length to match your physical door geometry.
