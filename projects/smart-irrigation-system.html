What You'll Build

A three-zone automatic irrigation controller built on an ESP32. Capacitive soil moisture sensors buried in each planting bed report moisture percentages to the board. When any zone drops below a configurable dry threshold (default 30 %), the ESP32 energises a corresponding 12 V solenoid valve via a relay module, watering until the sensor reads 80 %. All readings and valve states are pushed to a Blynk dashboard over Wi-Fi, giving you remote visibility and manual override from any smartphone.

How It Works

1

Sense Moisture

Three capacitive sensors output 0–3.3 V. analogRead() maps raw ADC counts to 0–100 % moisture after two-point calibration (air = 0 %, submerged = 100 %).

2

Threshold Logic

If moisture[i] < DRY_THRESHOLD, set relayPin[i] LOW (active-low relay) to open the solenoid. A hysteresis band prevents rapid cycling.

3

Cloud Push

Every 10 seconds, Blynk.virtualWrite() sends moisture values to V0–V2 gauge widgets. Manual relay override widgets allow tapping any valve open or closed remotely.

4

Safety Cutoffs

A maximum run-time timer (e.g., 5 min) per zone prevents flooding on sensor failure. An EEPROM daily water-budget counter logs total litres dispensed per day.
