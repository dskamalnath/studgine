Sound Level Meter

Sample ambient audio with a MAX4466 microphone amplifier and display a VU-meter bar graph on an LED array or OLED. Great for music visualisation and acoustic awareness.

📖 Introduction

A raw electret microphone produces signals in the µV–mV range — far too small for Arduino's 10-bit ADC to resolve usefully. The MAX4466 amplifier module boosts the microphone output by a factor of 25–125× (adjustable via an on-board gain pot), biasing the output centred at VCC/2 (2.5 V on a 5 V supply) so both positive and negative excursions of the audio waveform fall within the 0–5 V ADC range.

To detect volume (amplitude), you sample many cycles of the audio waveform rapidly, compute the peak-to-peak range of those samples, and map that to a dB-approximation. This is exactly how commercial VU meters and sound pressure level apps work.

⚙️ How It Works

  1. 1
    Wire MAX4466 — VCC → 3.3 V (or 5 V — check module spec), GND → GND, OUT → A0. Adjust the gain pot to a mid-position to start. The module's output idles at ~VCC/2; speak near it and the signal swings above and below that midpoint.
  2. 2
    Sample and measure amplitude — In a tight 50 ms window, take rapid samples and find the peak-to-peak range: unsigned long start = millis(); int lo=1023, hi=0; while(millis()-start < 50){int v=analogRead(A0); if(v<lo) lo=v; if(v>hi) hi=v;} int pp = hi - lo; Values of 0–40 are ambient noise; 200–600 is conversational; > 700 is loud music.
  3. 3
    Drive the VU bar — Map peak-to-peak to number of lit LEDs: int bars = map(pp, 0, 700, 0, 8);. Light bars 0 to bars-1, with the top bar optionally red (clipping indicator). Add a slow-fall peak-hold pointer by tracking the maximum bars value and decaying it by 1 every 200 ms.
  4. 4
    Add frequency bands (extension) — Sample at 8800 Hz using analogRead() (Arduino's max rate). Collect 64 samples into an array and run a simple 8-band filter bank using integer arithmetic (bin the magnitude of frequency groups). Update 8 columns of LEDs to create a primitive spectrum analyser — a classic advanced extension of this project.
