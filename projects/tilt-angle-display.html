Tilt Angle Display

Mount an MPU-6050 IMU over I2C, compute roll and pitch angles from raw accelerometer data with atan2, and display them live on an OLED screen.

📖 Introduction

The MPU-6050 is a 6-axis IMU (3-axis accelerometer + 3-axis gyroscope) that communicates over I2C. Its accelerometer measures the components of gravity along X, Y, Z axes. Using trigonometry — specifically atan2(y, z) for roll and atan2(-x, sqrt(y²+z²)) for pitch — you convert raw g-values into human-readable degrees.

This project is the gateway to drone flight controllers, robotic arm stabilisation, gesture-controlled cars, and wearable health trackers. Mastering IMU data feeds directly into intermediate and advanced projects.

⚙️ How It Works

  1. 1
    Wire the I2C bus — Both MPU-6050 (addr 0x68) and OLED (addr 0x3C) share the same SDA (A4) and SCL (A5) lines. Connect both VCCs to 3.3 V and GNDs to GND. MPU-6050's AD0 pin to GND selects address 0x68.
  2. 2
    Read accelerometer — Use the MPU6050 library: mpu.getMotion6(&ax,&ay,&az,&gx,&gy,&gz);. Scale raw values: float accelX = ax / 16384.0; (for ±2 g range). Now accelX, Y, Z are in units of g (gravity).
  3. 3
    Compute roll and pitch — float roll = atan2(accelY, accelZ) * 180.0 / PI;
    float pitch = atan2(-accelX, sqrt(accelY*accelY + accelZ*accelZ)) * 180.0 / PI; — This gives ±90° pitch and ±180° roll relative to gravity.
  4. 4
    Display on OLED — Clear the display each loop, then print: display.print("Roll: "); display.println(roll,1);. Use a complementary filter on the gyro data to smooth noisy readings: angle = 0.98*(angle + gyro*dt) + 0.02*accelAngle;
