📖 Introduction

Galvanic Skin Response (GSR), also called electrodermal activity (EDA), is the variation in the electrical conductance of skin caused by sweat gland activity. When you're stressed, nervous, or emotionally aroused, your sympathetic nervous system triggers more sweat secretion, which increases conductance by lowering the resistance between two skin contact points.

Commercial polygraphs measure GSR among other signals. Our simplified version measures the resistance between two fingertips via a voltage divider, converts it to a 10-bit ADC value, calibrates to your baseline, and categorises the relative change into calm, elevated, or stressed states.

Important disclaimer: This project is for educational exploration only. No Arduino-based device can accurately detect deception, and the results should never be treated as factual indicators of honesty.

⚙️ How It Works

  1. 1
    Build the voltage divider — Connect 5 V through a 10 kΩ resistor to one electrode and to Arduino A0. The second electrode connects to GND. The two finger contacts form a resistance bridge: V_out = 5V × R_skin / (10kΩ + R_skin). Skin resistance ranges roughly 10 kΩ (stressed) to 500 kΩ (relaxed), producing a voltage swing of ~0.1–2.5 V.
  2. 2
    Calibrate baseline — On startup, average 50 analogRead(A0) samples over 10 seconds while the subject breathes calmly: for(int i=0;i<50;i++){baseline+=analogRead(A0);delay(200);} baseline/=50; Store this as the neutral reference. Apply a running average: filtered = filtered * 0.9 + analogRead(A0) * 0.1; to smooth out 60 Hz mains noise.
  3. 3
    Calculate stress delta — Compute deviation from baseline: int delta = filtered - baseline;. A higher ADC value means lower skin resistance (more conductance = more stress). Use thresholds: delta < 15 → green LED (calm); 15–50 → yellow LED (mild arousal); > 50 → red LED (stressed). These thresholds are calibration-dependent — print delta to Serial Monitor to tune for your skin type.
  4. 4
    Display and extend — Print raw and delta values to Serial Plotter (Serial.println(delta);) — you'll see real-time waveforms as you relax and tense. Add an OLED to display a live bar graph, or a buzzer that increases in frequency proportionally to delta. Try adding a "reset calibration" button for multiple test subjects.
