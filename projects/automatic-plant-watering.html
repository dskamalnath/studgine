Automatic Plant Watering

A soil moisture sensor detects dry soil and automatically switches on a mini water pump via a relay module — your plant waters itself.

📖 Introduction

The capacitive soil moisture sensor v2.0 outputs an analog voltage proportional to the dielectric constant of the soil — dry soil (~3.2 V) vs wet soil (~1.5 V). The Arduino maps this reading and, when soil is too dry, drives a relay coil via a transistor to switch the 5–12 V pump circuit. A timed watering burst (e.g. 3 seconds) prevents over-watering.

This project is a practical IoT gateway: once it works on a breadboard you can extend it with an ESP8266 for remote monitoring, an RTC for scheduled watering, or a DHT11 to factor in ambient humidity.

⚙️ How It Works

  1. 1
    Wire the sensor — Sensor AOUT → A0, VCC → 3.3 V (not 5 V for capacitive type), GND → GND. Relay IN → D7, relay VCC → 5 V, relay GND → GND. Pump connects to the relay's COM/NO terminals with its own power supply.
  2. 2
    Calibrate — Insert the sensor in dry soil and note the reading (typically 600–800). Insert in very wet soil and note that reading (typically 200–400). Set DRY_THRESHOLD = 600. When analogRead(A0) > DRY_THRESHOLD, soil needs watering.
  3. 3
    Watering logic — When dry: digitalWrite(RELAY_PIN, LOW) (relay modules are often active-low), run pump for 3 seconds, then digitalWrite(RELAY_PIN, HIGH). Then wait 10 minutes before checking again to let water absorb.
  4. 4
    Add LCD feedback — Connect an I2C LCD (SDA→A4, SCL→A5). Display the raw moisture percentage and "Watering…" when the pump is active. Use the LiquidCrystal_I2C library to keep wiring simple.
