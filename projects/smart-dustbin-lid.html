Smart Dustbin Lid

An ultrasonic sensor detects a hand approaching and a servo automatically opens the bin lid — a hygienic, impressive build combining proximity sensing with servo actuation.

📖 Introduction

A smart touchless dustbin is one of the most satisfying beginner builds because it's genuinely useful and immediately impressive. An HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor on the lid measures the distance to the nearest object. When a hand comes within ~20 cm, a servo sweeps the lid open; after a hold timeout, it automatically closes again.

This project combines two skills you may have learned separately — ultrasonic distance measurement and servo control — into a functional product. It's also a great enclosure challenge: fitting everything inside a real bin or cardboard box teaches mechanical design thinking alongside electronics.

⚙️ How It Works

  1. 1
    Measure distance — Connect HC-SR04: VCC → 5 V, GND → GND, TRIG → pin 9, ECHO → pin 10. In a getDistance() function, pulse TRIG for 10 µs, time the ECHO pulse duration, and calculate: distance = duration * 0.034 / 2; in cm.
  2. 2
    Threshold detection — In loop(): if distance < 20 and the lid is not already open, sweep the servo to the open position (e.g. servo.write(90)) and record openedAt = millis(). Set lidOpen = true.
  3. 3
    Auto-close timer — Once the lid is open, keep checking millis() - openedAt. After a hold time (e.g. 3000 ms), call servo.write(0) to close and reset lidOpen = false. This ensures the lid closes even if nothing is thrown in.
  4. 4
    Mount and calibrate — Mount the servo arm to the lid with a rigid link or directly via a horn bracket. Adjust the open/close angles for your physical geometry — try 0°/90° first, then fine-tune. Hot-glue the ultrasonic sensor facing outward at the front of the lid for best detection.
