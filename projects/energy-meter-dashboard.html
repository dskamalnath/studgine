What You'll Build

A home energy meter that measures real AC power parameters using the PZEM-004T v3 module, which contains a built-in current transformer (CT) and shunt resistor. The ESP32 communicates with the PZEM over software serial using the Modbus RTU protocol. Readings — voltage, current, active power, power factor, frequency, and cumulative energy in kWh — are displayed on a local SSD1306 OLED. The ESP32 also transmits data to ThingSpeak or Blynk every 15 seconds, creating time-series graphs for energy audit and anomaly detection.

How It Works

1

Modbus Communication

PZEM-004T uses UART at 9600 baud with a Modbus RTU frame. The PZEM-004T-v30 library handles register reads. A 10 kΩ divider on PZEM TX protects the ESP32 3.3 V pin.

2

Read All Parameters

Call pzem.voltage(), pzem.current(), pzem.power(), pzem.energy(), pzem.pf(), and pzem.frequency() in a 1-second loop. Check for isnan() to detect sensor disconnection.

3

Display & Alert

Render a multi-page OLED display cycling every 3 seconds. Trigger a buzzer when power exceeds a user-set threshold, helping detect abnormal consumption spikes immediately.

4

Cloud Dashboard

Use ThingSpeak.writeFields() to upload 6 channels. Build a MATLAB Visualisation widget to compute daily cost in USD/MYR and alert monthly budget overruns via email.
